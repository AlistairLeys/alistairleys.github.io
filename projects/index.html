🏥 Healthcare Analytics: Diabetes Disease Progression Analysis

Technical Demonstration

End-to-end healthcare analytics workflow using the landmark diabetes dataset (Efron et al. 2004, 442 patients) to demonstrate machine learning model development, patient risk stratification, and clinical insight generation for disease progression prediction.

Data Science Solution

Machine learning model (Random Forest, R²=0.56 - strong performance for healthcare regression) predicting 12-month disease progression with validated statistical significance (p < 0.001). Three-segment patient clustering (Low/Moderate/High risk) with personalised care pathway recommendations. Feature importance analysis identifies S5 (35%), BMI (28%), and BP (18%) as key clinical indicators. Complete methodology documentation for technical and non-technical stakeholders.

Key Findings

29% of patients identified as high-risk requiring intensive intervention • 3 distinct risk clusters with tailored care recommendations • Model performance (R²=0.56) validated through 5-fold cross-validation • Feature importance analysis reveals S5 as strongest predictor (35% contribution) • Statistical significance confirmed (p < 0.001) across all key findings

Dataset: Efron et al. (2004), "Least Angle Regression," Annals of Statistics, 442 patients. This project demonstrates healthcare ML capabilities using real academic data for portfolio purposes.

Technical Excellence: Python, scikit-learn, Random Forest (R²=0.56), K-Means clustering, statistical validation, 5-fold cross-validation, feature importance analysis, academic citation (Efron et al. 2004), transparent methodology documentation, clinical insight visualisation

🚴‍♀️ Cyclistic Bike-Share Analysis

Business Challenge

Chicago bike-share company seeking to maximise annual memberships by understanding behavioural differences between casual riders and members to inform targeted conversion strategies with real pricing economics.

Analytical Approach

Comprehensive analysis of 499,176 real 2023 Chicago Divvy trips (balanced sample from 5.7M trip dataset) using Google Data Analytics Certificate methodology. Applied real Divvy pricing ($4.93 average casual ride vs. $143.90 annual membership), industry-benchmarked conversion rates (5-12%), and statistical validation to quantify revenue opportunity across four targeted strategies.

Demonstrated Business Impact

$4.3M total revenue potential identified • 29.2-ride break-even point (down from 34.3 with placeholder pricing) • $349.58 annual savings per conversion • Conservative conversion methodology documented for non-technical stakeholders • Four data-driven strategies targeting weekend riders, summer users, commuters, and frequent casual users

Technical Excellence: Python, Pandas, Statistical Analysis, Real Chicago Divvy Dataset (499,176 trips analysed from 5.7M records), Data Visualisation, Business Intelligence, ROI Quantification, Transparent Methodology Documentation
