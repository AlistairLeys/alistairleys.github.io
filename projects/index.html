🏥 Healthcare Analytics: NHS Diabetes Disease Progression & ROI Analysis

Business Challenge

NHS Trust partner (fictional scenario) managing 15,000 diabetes patients with rising costs (£119.7M annually) seeks to optimise £2.5M intervention budget and reduce disease progression by 25% through data-driven patient risk stratification and personalised care pathways.

Data Science Solution

Machine learning model (Random Forest, R²=0.56) predicting 12-month disease progression with validated statistical significance (p < 0.001). Three-segment patient clustering (Low/Moderate/High risk) with personalised care pathways. Feature importance analysis identifies S5 (35%), BMI (28%), and BP (18%) as key intervention targets. Complete business case with methodology documentation for non-technical NHS commissioners.

Demonstrated Business Impact

£39.15M net benefit over 3 years (2,900% ROI) • Break-even in Month 2 • £13.5M annual savings from proactive high-risk care (£9,400) vs. reactive complications (£12,500) • 29% of patients identified as high-risk requiring intensive intervention • 40% reduction in complications through early identification (evidence-based) • Conservative assumptions with best/base/worst case scenarios documented

Note: Fictional NHS scenario using real 442-patient sklearn dataset to demonstrate healthcare analytics and business case development capabilities for recruiters.

Technical Excellence: Python, scikit-learn, Random Forest (R²=0.56), K-Means clustering, statistical validation, 5-fold cross-validation, ROI quantification (£39.15M), NHS cost modelling, NICE alignment, non-technical stakeholder documentation, business impact visualisation

🚴‍♀️ Cyclistic Bike-Share Analysis

Business Challenge

Chicago bike-share company seeking to maximise annual memberships by understanding behavioural differences between casual riders and members to inform targeted conversion strategies with real pricing economics.

Analytical Approach

Comprehensive analysis of 5.7M real 2023 Chicago Divvy trips using Google Data Analytics Certificate methodology. Applied real Divvy pricing ($4.93 average casual ride vs. $143.90 annual membership), industry-benchmarked conversion rates (5-12%), and statistical validation to quantify revenue opportunity across four targeted strategies.

Demonstrated Business Impact

$4.3M total revenue potential identified • 29.2-ride break-even point (down from 34.3 with placeholder pricing) • $349.58 annual savings per conversion • Conservative conversion methodology documented for non-technical stakeholders • Four data-driven strategies targeting weekend riders, summer users, commuters, and frequent casual users

Technical Excellence: Python, Pandas, Statistical Analysis, Real Chicago Divvy Dataset (5.7M trips), Data Visualisation, Business Intelligence, ROI Quantification, Transparent Methodology Documentation
